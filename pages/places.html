Places To Visit

1. Pashupatinath Temple

Pashupatinath temple is one of the most sacred and ancient place to visit in Nepal. It is built in the 5th century. It is classified in world heritage site of UNESCO. Pashupatinate temple is locate in capital city of Nepal “Kathmandu”. Thousands of people come to visit Pashupatinath temple. According to the Hindu religion, Pashupatinath is considered as the protector of the universe and the patron deity of Nepali people.

2. Chitwan National Park

Chitwan National Park, established in 1973, was Nepal’s first National Park. Located in the Southern Central Terai of Nepal. Most popular place to visit of varities animals and birds. We can find one horn rhino, charial crocodiles, royal Bengal Tigers, Deer, Monkeys, Wild Boar and up to 544 species of birds. Chitwan National Park belongs to a World Heritage Site.

3. Mount Everest

The highest mountain on the earth. It is located in the between of Nepal and Tibet. Mount Everest is known as a dream destination for trekeers and indeed a life changing experience. The height of the mountain is 8848.86 meter. We can find rare animal in himilayan region such as black bear, red panda, musk deer, wolves.

4. Maya Devi temple

Maiya Devi Temple is the famous place to visit. It is main temple at Lumbine known as birth place of Lord Gautam Buddha. Lumbini is one of the holiest places of one of the world's great religions, and its remains contain important evidence about the nature of Buddhist pilgrimage centres from as early as the 3rd century B.C.

5. Kalinchowk

Kalinchowk is known as the snowy paradise of Nepal and is located 3842m above sea level in Dolakha district. Kalinchowk is a rural municipality, home to amazing scenery, cozy resorts on snowy hills, and the famous Kalinchowk Bhagwati Shrine. The distance between Kathmandu and Kalinchowk Bhagwati Shrine around 150 . In the winter season, especially in the months of December, January and February, the snowfall occurs. Many people visit there during the winter time to have some snow fun. The snowy visit and some friends along to play with, creates an amazing memory and quick relief from the monotony.

6. Bungee Jumping

In Nepal, you can bungee jump in two places: Bhote Koshi Gorge and Hemja, Pokhara. Bungee Jumping in Nepal is one of those activities that have gained wide popularity in the last few years at quite a fast rate. Many people either tourist or national people all of them participate in this activities and it is not too coasty so every one can fulfill their dream. Bhoti Kosi River gorge on the Nepal/Tibet border is the longest free-fall in the world of 500ft (160m).

7. Fewa Lake

Fewa Lake, is also known as Phewa Tal, located in the south of the Pokhara Valley that includes Pokhara city. Many tourist and local people come to visit fewa lake to worship goddess which is located in the middle of the lake called Tal Barahi. You can hire a boat, paddle at your own speed and having fun with family and friend. Pokhara is also the main tourist area. We can visit many places inside Pokhara. Like Davis Fall, Mahendra Cave, Bindhyabasini Temple etc.

8. Janaki Temple

Janaki Mandir is a Hindu temple in Janakpur, Nepal, dedicated to the Hindu goddess Sita. Every year, thousands of pilgrims from Nepal, India, Sri Lanka, and other countries visit Ram Janaki Temple to worship Lord Ram and Sita. Many worshippers visit the temple during the festivals of Ram Nawami, Vivaha Panchami, Dashain and Tihar.
